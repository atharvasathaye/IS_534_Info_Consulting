# Information Consulting Strategy: Aspen Pharmacare

## Overview
This repository contains the strategic consulting project materials for Aspen Pharmacare. The project focuses on identifying, analyzing, and resolving supply chain inefficiencies, specifically targeting ineffective demand forecasting and inventory management. Through comprehensive analysis, this project addresses the root causes of forecasting issues to improve Aspen Pharmacare's overall operational efficiency.

## Methodology
Our strategic approach involved a structured breakdown of the core problem into actionable components:
*   **Project Scoping:** Defining the core objectives and boundaries of the consulting engagement.
*   **Customer Journey Mapping:** Tracing the flow of operations and stakeholder experiences to pinpoint bottlenecks.
*   **Issue Tree Analysis:** Deconstructing the primary problem ("Ineffective Demand Forecasting") into root causes across three main areas:
    *   **Inaccurate predictive analytics:** Including limited historical data for AI training, lack of real-time data integration, and over-reliance on manual forecasting.
    *   **Poor seasonal demand anticipation:** Highlighting failures to adjust for medical demand spikes, lack of AI-driven trend recognition, and insufficient supplier coordination.
    *   **High stockouts and overstocking:** Resulting from slow responses to inventory fluctuations, inefficient reorder algorithms, and lack of real-time supply adjustments.
*   **Competitive Tech Assessments:** Evaluating existing technological solutions and proposing strategic upgrades for better forecasting.

## Key Findings
1.  **Data & Analytics Gaps:** A heavy reliance on manual forecasting methods combined with a lack of real-time data integration significantly hampers the accuracy of predictive models.
2.  **Responsiveness to Demand Variability:** The supply chain struggles to adjust to seasonal shifts and sudden medical demand spikes due to insufficient AI-driven trend recognition and supplier coordination.
3.  **Inventory Imbalances:** Inefficient reorder algorithms and slow response times lead to costly cycles of stockouts and overstocking, negatively impacting the bottom line.
